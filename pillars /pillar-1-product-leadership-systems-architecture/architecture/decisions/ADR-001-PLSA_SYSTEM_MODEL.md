# ADR-001 — Product Leadership Systems Architecture System Model

Status: Accepted  
Date: 2026-03-11

---

# Context

Modern product organizations struggle to translate strategic direction into coordinated product delivery and measurable customer outcomes.

Common challenges include:

• fragmented strategy execution  
• inconsistent portfolio governance  
• misalignment between delivery teams and strategic priorities  
• weak feedback loops between outcomes and leadership decisions  

Traditional product operating models often describe activities or team structures rather than a cohesive leadership architecture.

A formal architecture model was needed to:

- organize leadership responsibilities
- create consistent governance mechanisms
- connect strategy, delivery, and outcomes
- enable systematic decision making

---

# Decision

Define a **Product Leadership Systems Architecture (PLSA)** that models product leadership as a system of interacting operating structures.

The architecture organizes leadership responsibilities into a set of coordinated systems that translate strategic direction into execution and measurable outcomes.

The architecture emphasizes:

• structured leadership domains  
• explicit decision authority  
• coordinated interfaces between systems  
• continuous learning through feedback loops  

The PLSA model treats product leadership as an **operating architecture**, not merely a management practice.

---

# Rationale

A systems-based architecture provides several advantages.

First, it creates structural clarity across leadership responsibilities.  
Strategy, governance, execution, and outcomes are separated into explicit operating domains.

Second, it enables governance mechanisms that ensure strategic priorities translate into coordinated execution.

Third, it supports scalable product organizations where multiple teams operate within a shared architecture.

Finally, it enables architecture documentation, governance, and evolution similar to enterprise architecture frameworks.

---

# Consequences

Positive outcomes include:

• improved strategic alignment  
• clearer leadership accountability  
• more structured portfolio governance  
• stronger feedback loops between outcomes and strategy  

Potential constraints include:

• organizations must adopt structured governance practices  
• leadership teams must maintain consistent decision frameworks
