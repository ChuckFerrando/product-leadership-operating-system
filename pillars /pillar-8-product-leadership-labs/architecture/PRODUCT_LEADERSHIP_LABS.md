# Product Leadership Labs

The **Product Leadership Labs** defines **Pillar 8** of the **Product Leadership Operating System (PLOS)**.

It provides the **controlled exploration environment** through which new methods, operating patterns, and system enhancements are developed, tested, and validated **before being promoted into the canonical architecture**.

---

# Purpose

The purpose of **Product Leadership Labs** is to:

- enable disciplined experimentation within the operating system  
- explore new approaches without destabilizing the architecture  
- develop and refine emerging product leadership practices  
- test system extensions and improvements in a controlled environment  
- provide a structured path from experimentation to canonical adoption  

Labs ensures that the operating system can **evolve intentionally** without compromising its integrity.

---

# Position Within the Operating System

The **Product Leadership Operating System (PLOS)** explains how organizations operate across the full loop:

> **Strategy → Governance → Delivery → Outcomes → Learning → Strategy**

The system is composed of eight pillars:

1. Product Leadership Systems Architecture (PLSA)  
2. Product Leadership Operating Model  
3. Portfolio Governance System  
4. Product Delivery System  
5. Customer Outcomes System  
6. Decision Intelligence System  
7. Product Leadership Playbooks  
8. Product Leadership Labs *(this repository)*  

---

# What Labs Is

Labs is:

- a **controlled experimentation layer**  
- a **development environment for emerging methods**  
- a **pre-canonical staging area** for system evolution  
- a **structured exploration mechanism aligned to PLOS**  

Labs enables:

> **safe innovation without architectural instability**

---

# What Labs Is NOT

Labs is NOT:

- a production operating system  
- a decision-making authority  
- a replacement for any existing pillar  
- a mechanism to bypass governance or outcomes  
- a place for unstructured or ad hoc experimentation  

Labs must not introduce:

- new system responsibilities without validation  
- alternative operating loops  
- direct modifications to canonical pillars without promotion  

---

# Core Principle

> **All experimentation must be isolated from the canonical system until validated and formally promoted**

---

# System Boundaries (Non-Negotiable)

Labs must preserve all PLOS boundaries:

- **Decision Intelligence System** → signals, metrics, dashboards only  
- **Customer Outcomes System** → interpretation, evaluation, learning  
- **Governance / Strategy** → decisions and prioritization  
- **Delivery** → execution  
- **Playbooks** → execution patterns  

### Critical Rule

> **Labs must not collapse evidence, meaning, and decision-making into the same layer**

- Evidence remains in **Decision Intelligence System**  
- Meaning remains in **Customer Outcomes System**  
- Decisions remain in **Strategy & Governance**  

Labs may explore improvements to these systems, but must not merge or redefine them.

---

# Operating Model

Labs operates through a structured experimentation lifecycle:

## 1. Hypothesis Definition
- define the problem or opportunity  
- articulate the proposed approach  

## 2. Experiment Design
- define scope and boundaries  
- identify required inputs and systems involved  
- ensure alignment with PLOS constraints  

## 3. Execution
- run controlled experiments  
- capture observations and outputs  

## 4. Evaluation
- results are interpreted through the **Customer Outcomes System**  
- learning is generated within the Outcomes layer  

## 5. Promotion Decision
- governance determines whether the experiment:
  - is discarded  
  - is iterated  
  - is promoted into the canonical system  

---

# Promotion Model

No experimental artifact becomes canonical without formal promotion.

Promotion requires:

- validation through **Customer Outcomes System**  
- alignment with **PLOS architecture and boundaries**  
- governance approval  
- integration into the appropriate pillar  

Promotion paths include:

- playbooks (Pillar 7)  
- system enhancements (Pillars 1–6)  
- operating model updates (Pillar 2)  

---

# Interface Discipline

Labs interacts with the operating system through controlled interfaces:

- consumes inputs from existing systems  
- produces experimental outputs and observations  
- routes evaluation through **Customer Outcomes System**  
- routes decisions through **Governance**  

Mandatory mediation:

> **Decision Intelligence System → Customer Outcomes System → Strategy / Governance**

Labs must not introduce:

- direct signal-to-decision pathways  
- alternative evaluation mechanisms  
- bypass routes across systems  

---

# Artifact Types

Labs may produce:

- experimental playbooks  
- prototype operating models  
- test governance approaches  
- delivery coordination experiments  
- outcome evaluation refinements  
- decision intelligence enhancements  

All artifacts remain **non-canonical** until promoted.

---

# Relationship to Other Pillars

## Relationship to Pillar 7 — Product Leadership Playbooks

Labs may generate experimental playbooks.

Only validated playbooks may be promoted into Pillar 7.

---

## Relationship to Pillar 5 — Customer Outcomes System

All experiment evaluation and learning must occur within the **Customer Outcomes System**.

Labs does not evaluate outcomes directly.

---

## Relationship to Pillar 6 — Decision Intelligence System

Labs may use signals and metrics as inputs.

It must not interpret signals or assign meaning.

---

## Relationship to Pillar 3 — Portfolio Governance System

Governance determines:

- which experiments proceed  
- which experiments are promoted  
- which experiments are discontinued  

---

## Relationship to Pillar 2 — Product Leadership Operating Model

Labs may explore improvements to operating cadence and structure.

Adoption requires formal promotion.

---

# Summary

The **Product Leadership Labs** provides a controlled environment for evolving the **Product Leadership Operating System (PLOS)**.

It enables disciplined experimentation while preserving:

- system integrity  
- boundary enforcement  
- architectural consistency  

Labs ensures that innovation occurs **within structure**, not outside it.
