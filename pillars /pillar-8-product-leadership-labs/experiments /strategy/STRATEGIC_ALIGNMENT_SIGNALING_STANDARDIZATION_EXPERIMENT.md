# STRATEGIC_ALIGNMENT_SIGNALING_STANDARDIZATION_EXPERIMENT

## 2. Problem or Opportunity

Strategic direction is defined, but alignment across governance and delivery is inconsistent and slow.

---

## 3. Hypothesis

A standardized strategic alignment signaling pattern improves propagation and consistency of alignment.

---

## 4. Scope

- Affects: Strategy → Governance → Delivery  
- No change to strategy definition  

---

## 5. Inputs

- Strategy direction  
- Governance commitments  
- Delivery execution context  
- DI signals (uninterpreted)  

---

## 6. Experiment Design

- Introduce structured alignment signals at planning boundaries  
- Apply across 2 planning cycles  

---

## 7. Observations

- Faster alignment propagation observed  
- Reduced ambiguity in delivery interpretation  
- Consistent framing across governance and delivery  

---

## 8. Boundary Assessment

- No interpretation performed  
- No decisions made  
- Mediation path preserved  

---

## 9. Promotion Potential

- Target: Operating Model (P2)  
- Requires broader validation  

---

## 10. Review Outcome

**Candidate for Promotion Review**
