# Playbook Boundary Rule

Playbooks define how recurring work is executed.

They provide:
- execution sequences  
- facilitation patterns  
- templates and operating guidance  
- repeatable workflows  

---

## Boundary Constraints

Playbooks must not:

- make decisions  
- interpret Decision Intelligence signals  
- evaluate outcomes  
- create new system pathways  
- bypass Customer Outcomes  
- bypass Governance  
- contain experimentation as a canonical operating function  

---

## System Separation Rule

Playbooks operate strictly within execution boundaries.

They do not own:

- strategic direction (Strategy)  
- prioritization or tradeoff decisions (Governance)  
- outcome evaluation or learning (Customer Outcomes)  
- evidence interpretation (Decision Intelligence)  

---

## Experimentation Rule

All controlled experimentation belongs to:

> **Pillar 8 — Product Leadership Labs**

Playbooks may reference experiments, but must not:

- define experiments  
- run experiments  
- promote experiments  
- embed experimental logic into standard execution  

---

## Enforcement Principle

> Playbooks standardize execution — they do not redefine the operating system.

Any playbook that introduces:

- decision logic  
- evaluation logic  
- experimentation pathways  

must be corrected or rejected.
