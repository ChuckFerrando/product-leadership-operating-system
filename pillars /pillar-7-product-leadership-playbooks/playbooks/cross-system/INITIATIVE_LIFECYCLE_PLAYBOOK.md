# INITIATIVE_LIFECYCLE_PLAYBOOK

---

## 1. Context

Triggered when an initiative progresses across multiple systems in the PLOS loop.

Involves:

- Strategy  
- Governance  
- Delivery  
- Customer Outcomes  
- Decision Intelligence  

---

## 2. Objective

Ensure coordinated execution as initiatives move across the full PLOS lifecycle without breaking system boundaries.

---

## 3. Inputs

- Strategic direction (Strategy)  
- Approved priorities (Governance)  
- Delivery execution state (Delivery)  
- Outcome insights (Customer Outcomes)  
- Signals and telemetry (Decision Intelligence — signals only)  

---

## 4. Execution Steps

1. Align initiative scope to strategic direction  
2. Confirm governance approval and prioritization  
3. Coordinate delivery execution  
4. Maintain alignment across systems during execution  
5. Ensure outcomes are routed through Customer Outcomes  
6. Maintain visibility through Decision Intelligence signals  

---

## 5. Outputs

- Coordinated lifecycle execution  
- Cross-system alignment artifacts  
- Execution continuity across systems  

---

## 6. System Interactions

### Strategy
- Consumes direction  
- Does not redefine strategy  

### Governance
- Consumes decisions  
- Does not influence prioritization  

### Delivery
- Coordinates execution  

### Customer Outcomes
- Uses outcome insights  
- Does not evaluate outcomes  

### Decision Intelligence
- Uses signals  
- Does not interpret  

---

## 7. Guardrails (Non-Negotiable)

This playbook must NOT:

- collapse system boundaries  
- introduce direct DI → Governance/Strategy paths  
- interpret signals  
- evaluate outcomes  
- make decisions  

---

## 8. Failure Modes

- Boundary collapse across systems  
- Direct use of signals for decisions  
- Misalignment between systems  
- Skipping Customer Outcomes  

---

## 9. Signals of Execution Health

- Smooth transitions across systems  
- Consistent lifecycle flow  
- Alignment across strategy, governance, delivery, and outcomes  

**Critical Rule:**
- signals may be referenced  
- must NOT be interpreted
