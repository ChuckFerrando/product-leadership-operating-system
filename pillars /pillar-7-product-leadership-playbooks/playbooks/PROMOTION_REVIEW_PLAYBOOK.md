# Promotion Review Playbook

The **Promotion Review Playbook** defines the canonical execution pattern for how leadership teams prepare, run, document, and follow through on a promotion review within **Pillar 7 — Product Leadership Playbooks** of the **Product Leadership Operating System (PLOS)**.

Where the **Product Leadership Playbooks** pillar defines reusable execution patterns, this playbook specifies **how to conduct a disciplined promotion review** without changing system ownership boundaries across the operating system.

This playbook is intentionally procedural. It defines **how to run the review**, not **who owns performance evaluation logic**, **how outcomes are interpreted**, or **how strategic or governance decisions are made** elsewhere in PLOS.

---

## Purpose

Use this playbook to ensure promotion reviews are:

- structured
- evidence-based
- repeatable
- fair
- decision-ready
- well documented

The playbook exists to help leaders run promotion reviews with consistency and operational rigor while preserving clear separation between:

- **evidence collection**
- **evaluation and interpretation**
- **promotion decision forums**
- **follow-through actions**

---

## When To Use This Playbook

Use this playbook when a team needs to:

- review a promotion case for an individual contributor or manager
- prepare a promotion packet for a leadership or talent review forum
- standardize how promotion recommendations are assembled and discussed
- improve consistency across promotion cycles
- reduce ambiguity in promotion review preparation and follow-through

Do not use this playbook as a substitute for:

- compensation policy
- talent philosophy
- organizational design authority
- performance management frameworks
- career ladder definitions
- executive approval rights

---

## What This Playbook Covers

This playbook covers the operating flow for:

1. opening the promotion review cycle
2. confirming scope and candidate readiness
3. assembling evidence and supporting materials
4. validating packet completeness
5. facilitating the review discussion
6. capturing outcomes and follow-up actions
7. closing the cycle with documentation and communication

---

## Inputs

Typical inputs include:

- candidate name and role
- current level
- proposed level
- career framework or level guide
- manager recommendation
- calibrated evidence of scope, impact, and readiness
- relevant review notes
- timeline and decision forum date

---

## Outputs

Typical outputs include:

- complete promotion review packet
- review recommendation
- decision record
- follow-up actions
- communication plan
- archive-ready documentation for auditability and future reference

## Roles In The Playbook

Typical roles may include:

- hiring manager or people manager
- functional leader
- review facilitator
- HR or talent partner
- calibration participants
- approval authority

This playbook does **not** redefine formal authority. It only clarifies the operating sequence used to support the review.

## Playbook Flow

### 1. Open Review Window

Confirm:

- review cycle timing
- eligible candidates
- required materials
- review forum schedule
- approvers and participants
- deadlines and submission rules

Exit condition:

- the review window is formally opened and participants understand the process

### 2. Confirm Candidate Readiness

Validate that the candidate is appropriate for review based on the applicable career framework and promotion criteria.

Check for:

- sustained performance at the target scope
- evidence of level-appropriate behavior
- business impact
- organizational readiness for the role expectation
- manager support for nomination

Exit condition:

- the candidate is either advanced for packet preparation or held for a later cycle

### 3. Assemble Evidence Packet

Create a complete promotion packet using the organization’s defined template.

Typical packet sections include:

- candidate summary
- current role and target role
- scope of ownership
- impact evidence
- leadership evidence
- examples of decision quality and execution
- peer or partner input where applicable
- risks, gaps, or watchpoints

Guardrail:

- the packet should present evidence clearly, but it must not distort, inflate, or replace formal evaluation standards

Exit condition:

- the packet is complete enough for pre-review quality checking

### 4. Validate Packet Quality

Review the packet for completeness, clarity, comparability, and decision readiness.

Check for:

- missing evidence
- unclear claims
- unsupported impact statements
- role/level mismatch
- inconsistent terminology
- insufficient examples
- unresolved concerns that should be surfaced in advance

Exit condition:

- the packet is approved for the review forum or returned for revision

### 5. Run Promotion Review

Facilitate a disciplined discussion in the review forum.

Suggested sequence:

1. state the candidate and proposed promotion
2. summarize the case objectively
3. review scope and impact evidence
4. review level alignment
5. surface strengths, risks, and dissenting views
6. capture recommendation and rationale
7. confirm decision owner and next steps

Guardrail:

- this playbook governs the **process of discussion**, not the substantive authority model for the final promotion decision

Exit condition:

- the recommendation, rationale, and next actions are clearly captured

### 6. Record Decision And Actions

Document:

- decision status
- rationale
- follow-up requirements
- communication owner
- timing of communication
- any deferred or contingent actions

Possible statuses may include:

- approved
- approved pending condition
- deferred
- not approved in this cycle

Exit condition:

- the decision record is complete and follow-up ownership is clear

### 7. Communicate And Archive

Communicate the result through the appropriate leadership and HR channels.

Then archive:

- final packet
- decision summary
- action log
- final communication artifacts where appropriate

Exit condition:

- the cycle is closed with an auditable record and defined follow-through

## Operating Cadence

This playbook is usually run:

- on a defined promotion cycle
- in sync with talent review timing
- or ad hoc when an organization has an approved off-cycle process

The cadence may vary by company, but the operating sequence should remain stable.

---

## Guardrails

This playbook must NOT:

- redefine career architecture
- replace evaluation standards
- decide promotions outside the authorized forum
- create new governance authority
- interpret unrelated outcome signals
- bypass formal approval structure
- create hidden criteria outside the agreed promotion framework

Within PLOS, playbooks define **how to act**, not **what the operating system means** and not **who owns decisions beyond the playbook’s execution scope**.

---

## Relationship To The Operating System

Within PLOS:

- **Strategy** does not run promotion reviews
- **Governance** may own approval forums or talent governance, depending on organizational design
- **Delivery** is not the owner of promotion policy
- **Customer Outcomes** is not the owner of promotion process execution
- **Decision Intelligence** may supply reporting inputs, but it does not interpret candidate readiness or make promotion recommendations
- **Playbooks** define the reusable execution pattern for running the review
- **Labs** are not the path for granting promotion authority

This preserves the required separation between:

- evidence
- interpretation
- forum decision
- operational execution

- a standard operating guide for review facilitators

---

## Summary

The **Promotion Review Playbook** gives organizations a repeatable way to run promotion reviews with consistency, fairness, and operational discipline.

It improves execution quality without collapsing the boundaries between evidence, evaluation, and decision-making that the broader **Product Leadership Operating System (PLOS)** requires.
