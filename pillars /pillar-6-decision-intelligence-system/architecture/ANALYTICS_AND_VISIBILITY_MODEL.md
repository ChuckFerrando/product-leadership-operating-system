# Analytics and Visibility Model

The **Analytics and Visibility Model** defines the canonical structure through which the **Decision Intelligence System** presents **signals and metrics as observable system state** within the **Product Leadership Operating System (PLOS)**.

Where the **Metrics and Signal Model** defines how measurement is structured, and the **Measurement Integrity Model** ensures that measurement is trustworthy, this model defines how measurement is **made visible and accessible** without introducing interpretation, evaluation, or decision-making.

It establishes how dashboards, reporting systems, and analytical views provide **descriptive visibility** into system behavior while preserving strict separation from meaning and judgment.

---

# Purpose

This model defines how:

- signals and metrics are presented through dashboards and reporting  
- system behavior is made visible in a structured and consistent way  
- analytical views expose trends, distributions, and segmented observations of signals and metrics   
- visibility remains descriptive and non-evaluative  
- users can observe system state without embedding interpretation  

---

# Core Principle

> **Analytics shows what is happening — not what it means or what to do**

All analytics and visibility outputs are:

- descriptive  
- observable  
- non-evaluative  
- non-prescriptive  

---

# Model Structure

The Analytics and Visibility Model is composed of three core components:

> **Dashboards → Reporting → Analytical Views**

These components define how measurement is presented across different consumption contexts.

---

# 1. Dashboards

## Definition

Dashboards provide **real-time or near-real-time visibility** into system state through structured presentation of signals and metrics.

## Responsibilities

- displaying current system state  
- surfacing key metrics and signals  
- providing accessible visual summaries  

## Requirements

- must reflect underlying metrics accurately  
- must use consistent metric definitions  
- must maintain traceability to source metrics  

## Constraints

- must not interpret metrics  
- must not assign meaning or value  
- must not provide recommendations  
- must not imply success or failure  

---

# 2. Reporting

## Definition

Reporting provides **structured, time-based views** of signals and metrics for analysis and review.

## Responsibilities

- presenting historical trends  
- enabling comparison across time periods  
- organizing measurement into structured outputs  

## Requirements

- must maintain consistency of metric definitions over time  
- must preserve comparability across periods  
- must ensure traceability to source metrics  

## Constraints

- must not interpret trends  
- must not assign causality  
- must not embed evaluation logic  
- must not present conclusions  

---

# 3. Analytical Views

## Definition

Analytical views provide **deeper exploration of signals and metrics** through segmentation, breakdowns, and comparative analysis.

## Responsibilities

- enabling segmentation (e.g., by cohort, feature, system component)  
- exposing distributions and patterns  
- supporting exploratory analysis  

## Requirements

- must operate on defined signals and metrics  
- must preserve measurement consistency  
- must maintain traceability  

## Constraints

- must not infer meaning  
- must not determine value realization  
- must not produce evaluative outputs  
- must not generate recommendations
- must not identify or imply causal relationships  

---

# Cross-Cutting Requirements

All analytics and visibility outputs must adhere to:

## 1. Accuracy
- outputs must reflect underlying metrics exactly  

## 2. Consistency
- all views must use standardized metric definitions  

## 3. Traceability
- all outputs must be traceable to metrics and signals  

## 4. Transparency
- calculation logic and data sources must be visible  

## 5. Non-Interpretation
- no output may embed meaning, value, or evaluation  

---

# Relationship to Metrics and Signals

- dashboards → display metrics and signals  
- reporting → organizes metrics over time  
- analytical views → explore metrics and signals  

This ensures:

> **all visibility is grounded in structured, non-interpretive measurement**

---

# Relationship to the Customer Outcomes System

The Customer Outcomes System is the **only system allowed to interpret analytics outputs**.

- Analytics → provides **visibility**  
- Outcomes → produces **meaning, evaluation, and learning**  

This ensures:

> **visibility does not become interpretation**

---

# Relationship to Strategy and Governance

- Strategy and Governance may **observe dashboards and reports**  
- they must not act directly on them  

All action must be based on:

- evaluated outcomes  
- structured learning  

---

# Failure Modes Prevented

This model prevents:

- dashboards becoming decision tools  
- metrics being interpreted as outcomes  
- reports embedding conclusions  
- analytics generating recommendations  
- organizations reacting directly to visible trends  
- confusion between observation and evaluation  

---

# How to Use This Model

- use dashboards to observe current system state  
- use reporting to review historical behavior  
- use analytical views to explore patterns  
- use the Customer Outcomes System to interpret meaning  
- use Governance and Strategy to act on evaluated learning  

---

# Relationship to the Operating System

The Analytics and Visibility Model strengthens the Product Leadership Operating System by ensuring that:

- system behavior is visible  
- measurement is accessible  
- analysis is structured  

While preserving:

> **strict separation between visibility, interpretation, and decision-making**

---

# Summary

The **Analytics and Visibility Model** defines how measurement is presented within the Decision Intelligence System.

It ensures that:

- dashboards, reports, and analytical views provide structured visibility  
- measurement remains accurate and consistent  
- visibility remains descriptive and non-evaluative  

While enforcing:

> **No interpretation. No evaluation. No decisions.**
