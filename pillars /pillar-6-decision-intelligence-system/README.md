# Decision Intelligence System

This repository documents **Pillar 6** of the **Product Leadership Operating System (PLOS)** and defines how organizations generate, structure, and govern **signals, metrics, dashboards, analytics, and measurement visibility** to support decision-making across the operating system without owning interpretation, evaluation, or decisions.

Where the **Product Leadership Systems Architecture (PLSA)** defines the canonical five-system architecture, the **Decision Intelligence System** defines the system responsible for making organizational behavior observable through structured measurement and analytical visibility.

Its role is to support the operating loop with **evidence** — not meaning, judgment, prioritization, or control.

---

# Purpose

This repository defines the **canonical Decision Intelligence System** within the Product Leadership Operating System.

It explains:

- how signals and metrics are generated and structured  
- how measurement systems are defined and governed  
- how dashboards and analytics provide visibility into system behavior  
- how measurement integrity and traceability are maintained  
- how Decision Intelligence supports the operating system without assuming decision authority  

---

# Position in the Operating System

The **Decision Intelligence System** operates as a **supporting system across the full PLOS loop**:

> **Strategy → Governance → Delivery → Outcomes → Learning → Strategy**

It enables:

- **Strategy** with structured measurement definitions  
- **Governance** with portfolio visibility  
- **Delivery** with execution telemetry  
- **Outcomes** with signals used for interpretation and evaluation  

It does **not** participate as a decision-making step in the loop.

Learning generated by the Customer Outcomes System closes the loop by informing Strategy through validated outcome understanding, not raw measurement.

---

# Core Responsibility

The **Decision Intelligence System** is responsible for:

- generating **signals and observable indicators**  
- defining and governing **metrics and measurement frameworks**  
- providing **dashboards and reporting visibility**  
- enabling **analytics and descriptive visibility** 
- ensuring **measurement integrity, consistency, and traceability**  

It ensures that the operating system is **observable, measurable, and evidence-driven**.

---

# Critical Boundary

> **Decision Intelligence supports — it does not interpret, evaluate, or decide**

The system produces **evidence**, not **meaning**.

The Decision Intelligence System does **not**:

- interpret signals  
- qualify value  
- evaluate outcomes  
- define strategic meaning  
- make governance decisions  
- prioritize work  
- control delivery execution  
- trigger interventions  

All interpretation, value qualification, evaluation, and learning belong to the **Customer Outcomes System**.

All decisions belong to **Strategy** and **Governance**.

---

# Relationship to Other Systems

## Customer Outcomes System (Pillar 5)

The Customer Outcomes System is the required interpretation and evaluation layer through which all Decision Intelligence outputs must pass before influencing Strategy or Governance.

- Decision Intelligence provides → **signals, metrics, dashboards, analytical visibility**  
- Customer Outcomes provides → **interpretation, value qualification, evaluation, and learning**  

This separation ensures:

> **Evidence and meaning are never conflated**

---

## Portfolio Governance System (Pillar 3)

- Governance may **observe measurement outputs**  
- Governance must **not act directly on raw signals or dashboards**  

All governance action must be based on:

- evaluated outcomes  
- structured learning  
- framed intervention inputs  

---

## Strategy Execution System (Pillar 1)

- Strategy defines **measurement expectations**  
- Decision Intelligence enables **measurement visibility**  

Strategy must not refine itself directly from raw dashboards or unevaluated signals.

---

## Product Delivery System (Pillar 4)

- Delivery produces **execution telemetry and observable effects**  
- Decision Intelligence captures and structures this data as signals  

Delivery does not determine whether value was realized.

# Core Capabilities

## Metrics & Signal Definition
Defines how signals and metrics are structured, including:

- strategic metrics  
- delivery metrics  
- outcome signals  
- operational indicators  

---

## Signal Generation
Produces observable evidence of system behavior, including:

- usage signals  
- behavioral signals  
- performance signals  
- system health indicators  

Signals represent **raw evidence only**.

---

## Analytics & Visibility
Provides structured visibility through:

- dashboards  
- reporting layers  
- trend analysis  
- segmentation and comparative views  

Outputs are **descriptive**, not evaluative.

---

## Measurement Integrity
Ensures:

- metric consistency  
- data accuracy  
- definition stability  
- traceability across systems  
- controlled evolution of measurement frameworks  

---

## Instrumentation & Telemetry
Defines how system activity becomes observable through:

- instrumentation design  
- telemetry pipelines  
- data capture and flow  

---

# Interfaces

The Decision Intelligence System interacts with other systems through **strict, non-overlapping interfaces**.

## Outbound

- **Decision Intelligence → Outcomes**  
  - signals  
  - metrics  
  - dashboards  
  - analytical outputs  

- **Decision Intelligence → Strategy / Governance / Delivery**  
  - visibility only  

---

## Inbound

- **Outcomes → Decision Intelligence**  
  - measurement gaps  
  - signal refinement needs  
  - instrumentation requirements  

- **Delivery → Decision Intelligence**  
  - telemetry and execution data  

- **Strategy / Governance → Decision Intelligence**  
  - measurement requirements  
  - metric definitions  

---

# Failure Modes

Common failure modes this system is designed to prevent:

- dashboards used as decision engines  
- metrics interpreted outside the Outcomes system  
- governance reacting directly to raw signals  
- strategy driven by unevaluated data  
- analytics presented as conclusions instead of evidence
