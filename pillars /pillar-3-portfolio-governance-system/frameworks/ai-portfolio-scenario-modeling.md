# AI-Assisted Portfolio Scenario Modeling

This document outlines how AI tools can assist executive leadership in evaluating portfolio allocation scenarios, tradeoffs, and strategic outcomes.

AI augments human judgment; it does not replace governance authority.

---

# I. Purpose

AI-assisted modeling supports:

- Rapid scenario exploration
- Capital reallocation impact analysis
- Portfolio balance stress testing
- Executive narrative preparation

The objective is improved decision clarity under complexity.

---

# II. Inputs

AI scenario analysis draws from structured portfolio artifacts:

- Portfolio Scoring Matrix
- Risk Scoring Model
- Capital Allocation Model
- Investment Memos
- Portfolio Heatmap

These inputs ensure analysis remains grounded in governance frameworks.

---

# III. Scenario Types

## Capital Reallocation
Evaluate impact of shifting investment between initiatives.

Example:
- Reallocate 10% capital from operational efficiency to strategic expansion.

## Risk Stress Testing
Simulate portfolio impact if risk scores increase for critical initiatives.

Example:
- Dependency failure in a Tier 1 initiative.

## Capacity Constraints
Model impact of reduced delivery capacity.

Example:
- Engineering capacity reduced by 15%.

## Strategic Pivot
Assess portfolio alignment after strategy change.

Example:
- Increased emphasis on platform resilience.

---

# IV. AI Workflow

1. Gather portfolio data from governance artifacts.
2. Structure inputs into scenario parameters.
3. Generate scenario analysis using AI assistance.
4. Validate outputs through portfolio governance review.
5. Present results in executive narrative format.

AI-generated analysis must always be reviewed by portfolio leadership.

---

# V. Outputs

Typical outputs include:

- Scenario comparison summaries
- Portfolio heatmap projections
- Capital allocation shift analysis
- Executive decision briefs

Outputs must remain concise and decision-oriented.

---

# VI. Governance Safeguards

AI-generated recommendations must:

- Reference underlying scoring models
- Document assumptions
- Avoid autonomous decision authority

All final decisions remain with executive leadership.

---

# VII. Example Use Case

Scenario:
Engineering capacity drops by 20%.

AI analysis may highlight:
- Initiatives most vulnerable to delay
- Candidates for deferral
- Portfolio balance adjustments

Leadership then determines appropriate tradeoffs.
