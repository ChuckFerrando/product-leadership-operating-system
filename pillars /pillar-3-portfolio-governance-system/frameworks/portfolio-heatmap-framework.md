# Portfolio Heatmap Framework

The portfolio heatmap provides a visual representation of investment distribution, initiative health, and strategic alignment across the product and technology portfolio.

The objective is rapid executive comprehension of portfolio status.

---

# I. Heatmap Purpose

The heatmap enables leadership to quickly assess:

- Portfolio investment distribution
- Initiative health and execution risk
- Strategic alignment coverage
- Delivery predictability

It converts portfolio complexity into a structured visual signal.

---

# II. Heatmap Axes

The heatmap uses two primary axes.

**Horizontal Axis – Strategic Alignment**

Measures the degree to which initiatives support enterprise priorities.

Low → Limited strategic contribution  
High → Direct advancement of strategic objectives

**Vertical Axis – Execution Risk**

Derived from the composite risk scoring model.

Low → Stable delivery  
High → Elevated delivery risk

---

# III. Quadrant Interpretation

| Quadrant | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| High Alignment / Low Risk | Priority investments |
| High Alignment / High Risk | Strategic bets requiring oversight |
| Low Alignment / Low Risk | Operational optimization |
| Low Alignment / High Risk | Candidates for de-prioritization |

---

# IV. Initiative Representation

Each initiative appears as a point on the heatmap.

Additional attributes may include:

- Bubble size → Capital allocation
- Color → Portfolio tier
- Shape → Initiative category (sustainment / strategic / efficiency / innovation)

---

# V. Portfolio Balance Indicators

Leadership should monitor:

- Concentration of investments in single quadrants
- Overexposure to high-risk initiatives
- Underinvestment in strategic capability expansion
- Portfolio dependency clusters

Portfolio balance is reviewed quarterly.

---

# VI. Governance Integration

The heatmap is presented during:

- Quarterly portfolio review
- Capital allocation planning
- Executive delivery assessments

Heatmap shifts should be explained and documented.

---

# VII. Example Visualization (Conceptual)

Example distribution:

High Alignment / Low Risk  
→ Core strategic initiatives

High Alignment / High Risk  
→ Innovation or transformational programs

Low Alignment / Low Risk  
→ Sustaining engineering

Low Alignment / High Risk  
→ Portfolio cleanup candidates
