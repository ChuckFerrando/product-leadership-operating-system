# Risk Scoring Model

This document defines the structured risk evaluation framework used to assess portfolio initiatives.

Risk scoring informs capital allocation, tiering, and stage-gate decisions.

The objective is explicit risk acknowledgment — not risk avoidance.

---

# I. Risk Philosophy

1. Risk must be quantified before capital is released.
2. High-impact initiatives may carry high risk — but risk must be visible.
3. Unacknowledged risk is unacceptable.
4. Mitigation plans are mandatory for scores below defined thresholds.

---

# II. Risk Dimensions

Each initiative is evaluated across five categories.

All dimensions use a 1–5 scale (5 = low risk, 1 = high risk).

| Risk Category | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Technical Risk | Architecture complexity, integration uncertainty, scalability constraints |
| Operational Risk | Dependency exposure, process readiness, delivery predictability |
| Compliance & Regulatory Risk | Legal, contractual, cybersecurity, or regulatory exposure |
| Mission Dependency Risk | Impact to mission-critical operations if failure occurs |
| Supply Chain / External Dependency Risk | Vendor concentration, external integration reliance |

---

# III. Risk Scoring Criteria

## 1. Technical Risk

5 — Proven architecture, minimal unknowns  
4 — Minor integration complexity  
3 — Moderate architectural evolution  
2 — Significant unknowns or refactoring  
1 — High uncertainty or unproven capability  

---

## 2. Operational Risk

5 — Self-contained delivery with mature teams  
4 — Minor cross-functional dependencies  
3 — Moderate dependency complexity  
2 — Significant cross-team coordination required  
1 — Multi-portfolio or enterprise-wide dependency exposure  

---

## 3. Compliance & Regulatory Risk

5 — No regulatory exposure  
4 — Routine compliance controls  
3 — Moderate compliance validation required  
2 — High regulatory scrutiny or security review  
1 — Material compliance exposure or contractual risk  

---

## 4. Mission Dependency Risk

5 — Non-critical capability  
4 — Limited operational disruption if delayed  
3 — Noticeable operational impact  
2 — Critical mission dependency  
1 — Failure creates operational degradation  

---

## 5. Supply Chain / External Risk

5 — No external dependencies  
4 — Redundant vendor support  
3 — Moderate third-party reliance  
2 — Concentrated vendor dependency  
1 — Single point of external failure  

---

# IV. Composite Risk Score

Composite Risk Score = Average of all five dimensions.

Interpretation:

4.5 – 5.0 → Low Risk  
3.5 – 4.49 → Managed Risk  
2.5 – 3.49 → Elevated Risk  
< 2.5 → High Risk (Executive acknowledgment required)

---

# V. Risk Mitigation Requirements

For initiatives scoring below 3.5:

- Formal mitigation plan required
- Identified risk owner
- Defined review cadence
- Explicit stage-gate control points

For initiatives scoring below 2.5:

- Executive-level sign-off required
- Documented acceptance rationale

---

# VI. Integration with Portfolio Scoring

Risk score feeds into:

- Weighted portfolio scoring model
- Tier assignment
- Stage-gate capital release decisions
- Board-level reporting

Risk transparency strengthens allocation integrity.

---

# VII. Auditability & Traceability

Each initiative must retain:

- Risk scoring worksheet
- Mitigation documentation
- Executive acknowledgment (if required)
- Updated risk score at each stage gate
