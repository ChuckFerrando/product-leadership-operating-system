# Portfolio Scoring Matrix

This document defines the quantitative evaluation model used to prioritize, compare, and govern portfolio investments.

The objective is disciplined, repeatable capital allocation across mission-critical and enterprise environments.

---

# I. Scoring Overview

Each initiative is evaluated across five weighted dimensions.

All scores use a 1–5 scale.

Final score is calculated as:

Weighted Score = Σ (Dimension Score × Weight)

Maximum possible score = 5.0

---

# II. Scoring Dimensions & Weights

| Dimension | Weight | Description |
|------------|--------|------------|
| Strategic Alignment | 30% | Direct linkage to enterprise or mission priority |
| Mission / Business Impact | 25% | Measurable operational or capability uplift |
| Risk Profile (Inverse) | 20% | Technical, operational, compliance exposure |
| Resource Efficiency | 15% | Capital and talent efficiency relative to impact |
| Time to Impact | 10% | Speed of measurable value realization |

Weights may be adjusted annually during strategic planning cycles.

---

# III. Dimension Scoring Criteria

## 1. Strategic Alignment (30%)

5 — Directly advances a top enterprise or mission priority  
4 — Strong alignment to a defined strategic objective  
3 — Indirect or secondary strategic support  
2 — Weak alignment  
1 — Minimal strategic linkage  

---

## 2. Mission / Business Impact (25%)

5 — Material enterprise-level impact  
4 — Significant portfolio-level impact  
3 — Moderate operational improvement  
2 — Incremental improvement  
1 — Marginal benefit  

Impact must be documented with measurable outcomes.

---

## 3. Risk Profile (20%)

Risk is scored inversely (lower risk = higher score).

5 — Low technical and operational risk  
4 — Manageable risk with mitigation plan  
3 — Moderate risk with dependencies  
2 — High risk requiring executive oversight  
1 — Material mission or compliance exposure  

High-risk initiatives require explicit acknowledgment.

---

## 4. Resource Efficiency (15%)

5 — High impact relative to low capital requirement  
4 — Strong ROI profile  
3 — Balanced cost-to-impact  
2 — Capital intensive relative to impact  
1 — Disproportionate resource requirement  

---

## 5. Time to Impact (10%)

5 — < 1 quarter to measurable value  
4 — 1–2 quarters  
3 — 2–4 quarters  
2 — > 4 quarters  
1 — Indeterminate timeline  

---

# IV. Score Interpretation Bands

| Weighted Score | Interpretation | Governance Action |
|----------------|---------------|------------------|
| 4.2 – 5.0 | Priority Investment | Fund and monitor at Tier 1 |
| 3.5 – 4.19 | Strategic Candidate | Fund within portfolio constraints |
| 2.8 – 3.49 | Conditional | Requires scope refinement or risk mitigation |
| < 2.8 | Deprioritize | Do not allocate capital |

Thresholds are reviewed annually.

---

# V. Portfolio Balance Overlay

Scores alone do not determine allocation.

Final decisions must consider:

- Category allocation targets (Sustainment / Strategic / Efficiency / Innovation)
- Portfolio concentration risk
- Cross-initiative dependencies
- Capital availability

This prevents score-driven distortion.

---

# VI. Governance Integration

Scoring is required at:

- Initial investment proposal
- Stage-gate transitions
- Quarterly portfolio review

Score changes must be documented.

No initiative advances tiers without updated scoring.

---

# VII. Auditability

Each scored initiative must retain:

- Completed scoring worksheet
- Supporting rationale
- Risk mitigation plan
- Executive decision record

Scoring transparency is mandatory.
