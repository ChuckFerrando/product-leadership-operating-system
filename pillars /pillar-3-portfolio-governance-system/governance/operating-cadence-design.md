# Operating Cadence Design

This document defines the governance rhythm that translates strategy into predictable execution.

The cadence is designed to:
- Reduce decision latency
- Maintain portfolio integrity
- Surface risks early
- Produce board-ready visibility without operational drag

---

# I. Cadence Objectives

1. **Decision velocity**: decisions happen at the lowest appropriate level, quickly.
2. **Portfolio coherence**: investments remain aligned to strategy and capacity.
3. **Risk visibility**: risks are identified, owned, and mitigated with clear triggers.
4. **Narrative clarity**: consistent executive messaging supported by measurable signals.
5. **Auditability**: decisions and tradeoffs are documented and traceable.

---

# II. Governance Forums

## Weekly — Execution Checkpoint (60 minutes)
**Participants:** Initiative owners, delivery leads, portfolio ops  
**Purpose:** Manage delivery flow, dependencies, blockers  
**Inputs:**
- Top risks / blockers
- Dependency changes
- Release readiness status
**Outputs:**
- Decisions made at operating level
- Escalations (if required)
- Updated risk notes

---

## Biweekly — Portfolio Health Review (60–90 minutes)
**Participants:** Product/Eng/Design/Finance leaders (portfolio scope)  
**Purpose:** Maintain portfolio integrity and delivery predictability  
**Inputs:**
- Portfolio heatmap deltas
- Scoring changes (if any)
- Risk score changes + mitigation status
- Capacity shifts
**Outputs:**
- Tier changes (promote/demote)
- Scope adjustment decisions
- Resource tradeoff recommendations

---

## Monthly — Investment & Tradeoff Review (90 minutes)
**Participants:** Exec sponsors, Finance, portfolio governance owner  
**Purpose:** Capital allocation adjustments and decision-rights execution  
**Inputs:**
- New investment memos
- Stage-gate transitions requiring funding release
- Benefits realization check
- Concentration risk indicators
**Outputs:**
- Go / Adjust / Stop decisions
- Capital released (stage-gate based)
- Tradeoffs logged with rationale

---

## Quarterly — Portfolio Review / QBR (2 hours)
**Participants:** Executive leadership; board visibility as applicable  
**Purpose:** Strategic alignment validation and portfolio posture  
**Inputs:**
- Portfolio heatmap (current + trend)
- Category balance (sustainment/strategic/efficiency/innovation)
- Top 5 risks + mitigation status
- Delivery predictability indicators
**Outputs:**
- Confirmed priorities
- Strategic rebalancing decisions
- Board-ready narrative and asks

---

## Annual — Strategic Planning & Capital Allocation (half-day + follow-ups)
**Participants:** Executive leadership, Finance, portfolio governance  
**Purpose:** Set strategy-to-funding plan and operating targets  
**Inputs:**
- Strategic priorities
- Capacity and budget constraints
- Prior-year outcomes
- Risk posture and resilience requirements
**Outputs:**
- Annual allocation targets by category
- Tier 1 initiative list
- Governance calendar for the year

# IV. Escalation Triggers

Escalate from operating level to portfolio governance when:

- Composite risk score falls below threshold
- Delivery milestone slips beyond agreed tolerance
- Scope increases beyond tier limits
- Dependencies shift materially
- Capital request exceeds stage-gate envelope
- Compliance/security review flags material risk

Escalation should include:
- The decision required
- Options considered
- Recommendation
- Deadline

---

# V. Decision Documentation

Every forum produces decisions.

Minimum decision record:
- Decision statement
- Owner
- Date
- Rationale
- Tradeoffs
- Follow-up actions

Unlogged decisions are treated as non-decisions.

---

# VI. Operating Metrics (Minimal Set)

Metrics are selected to support decision-making, not reporting volume.

Recommended minimal set:
- Delivery predictability (planned vs actual)
- Portfolio throughput (initiatives completed per quarter by tier)
- Risk trend (composite risk distribution over time)
- Capital release vs plan (stage-gate)
- Benefits realized vs expected
