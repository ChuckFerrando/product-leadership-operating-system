# Operating Model Principles

This document defines the foundational architecture for translating strategy into governed execution.

---

## 1. Governance as Acceleration
Governance mechanisms exist to reduce decision latency, not introduce control drag.

Clear decision rights, defined stage gates, and explicit capital allocation models increase velocity.

---

## 2. Strategy Decomposition
Strategy must decompose into:

- Portfolio priorities
- Tiered initiatives
- Resource allocation
- Measurable outcomes

Ambiguity compounds execution risk.

---

## 3. Decision Rights Clarity
Decision-making authority must be explicit across:

- Product
- Engineering
- Finance
- Executive leadership

Undefined ownership creates operational friction.

---

## 4. Capital Allocation Discipline
Investment decisions are portfolio decisions.

All initiatives must be evaluated against:

- Strategic alignment
- Expected return
- Risk profile
- Opportunity cost

---

## 5. Operating Cadence
Execution requires predictable rhythm:

- Quarterly investment reviews
- Monthly portfolio health assessments
- Weekly execution checkpoints
