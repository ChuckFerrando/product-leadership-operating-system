# ADR-002 — Five-System Product Leadership Architecture

Status: Accepted  
Date: 2026-03-11

---

# Context

The Product Leadership Systems Architecture required a clearly defined system model to organize leadership responsibilities and maintain architectural consistency.

Without a defined system structure, the architecture could drift into loosely connected frameworks.

The architecture required a stable set of operating systems that would:

• define leadership domains  
• organize decision authority  
• connect strategy to execution  
• enable customer outcome evaluation  

---

# Decision

Define the Product Leadership Systems Architecture using **five canonical systems**:

1. Strategy Execution System  
2. Portfolio Governance System  
3. Product Delivery System  
4. Customer Outcomes System  
5. Decision Intelligence System

These systems form the complete operating model of the architecture.

---

# Rationale

The five-system structure provides a balanced representation of product leadership responsibilities.

Strategy Execution ensures organizations translate long-term strategic direction into actionable priorities.

Portfolio Governance ensures investment decisions and delivery capacity align with strategic intent.

Product Delivery coordinates the execution of product initiatives and operational work.

Customer Outcomes measures the real-world impact of product investments and informs leadership learning.

Decision Intelligence provides analytical insight and decision support across all systems.

This structure preserves clear boundaries while enabling coordinated operation.

---

# Consequences

Benefits include:

• consistent leadership operating model  
• structured portfolio governance  
• clear separation of strategic and operational responsibilities  
• measurable outcomes and learning loops  

Constraints include:

• architecture extensions must preserve the five-system model  
• new artifacts must align with existing system boundaries
