# Product Leadership Systems Architecture — System Diagnostic Scorecard

The System Diagnostic Scorecard provides a practical assessment tool for evaluating the health and maturity of the Product Leadership Systems Architecture (PLSA).

It is designed to help leaders assess how effectively the operating system is functioning across strategy execution, portfolio governance, product delivery, customer outcomes, and decision intelligence. Rather than relying on informal impressions, the scorecard introduces a structured method for identifying strengths, weaknesses, and improvement priorities across the leadership system.

The scorecard is intended to translate the architecture into a usable diagnostic instrument that supports operating model review, maturity assessment, and continuous improvement.

---

## Purpose

The purpose of the System Diagnostic Scorecard is to provide a repeatable framework for assessing the effectiveness of the Product Leadership Systems Architecture in practice.

It is intended to help leaders:

- identify strengths and weaknesses across the operating model
- evaluate system maturity and structural coherence
- diagnose breakdowns between strategy, governance, delivery, and outcomes
- prioritize operating model improvement efforts
- support recurring leadership reviews and transformation work

This scorecard should be used as a practical companion to the architecture, framework, diagram, and playbook documents in the repository.

---

## Scorecard Structure

The scorecard is organized around the five coordinated operating systems of the Product Leadership Systems Architecture:

1. Strategy Execution System
2. Portfolio Governance System
3. Product Delivery System
4. Customer Outcomes System
5. Decision Intelligence System

Each section includes a set of diagnostic criteria that can be scored using the following scale:

| Score | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 1 | Ad hoc or largely absent |
| 2 | Emerging but inconsistent |
| 3 | Structured and repeatable |
| 4 | Integrated across the operating model |
| 5 | Optimized and continuously refined |

The goal is not to produce a superficial maturity label, but to identify where the operating system is coherent, where it is fragmented, and where improvement effort should be concentrated.

## Scoring Guidance

The scorecard should be completed based on observable organizational behavior rather than aspirational intent.

Use the following guidance when scoring:

- **1 — Ad hoc or largely absent:** The capability is weak, inconsistent, informal, or not meaningfully present.
- **2 — Emerging but inconsistent:** The capability exists in partial form but is uneven, dependent on individuals, or unreliable.
- **3 — Structured and repeatable:** The capability is defined, visible, and used with reasonable consistency.
- **4 — Integrated across the operating model:** The capability is not only present, but connected to other systems in a disciplined way.
- **5 — Optimized and continuously refined:** The capability is strong, adaptive, evidence-based, and continuously improved.

The score should reflect current operating reality, not desired future state.

---

## Diagnostic Interpretation

The scorecard should be interpreted as a system-level diagnostic rather than a compliance exercise.

Low scores in one area often indicate weaknesses in connected systems. For example:

- weak strategy translation may cause poor portfolio prioritization
- weak governance may create delivery overload and fragmented execution
- weak outcome visibility may prevent meaningful strategic refinement
- weak decision intelligence may reduce the quality of leadership intervention across the model

For that reason, the scorecard should be used to identify patterns across the operating system, not just isolated capability gaps.

It is often more useful to compare clusters of scores than to focus only on averages. A structurally fragmented operating model may show pockets of strength but still perform poorly overall because the systems are not coherently connected.

## System Explanation

The scorecard evaluates all five systems in the Product Leadership Systems Architecture.

### Strategy Execution System

This section assesses whether the organization has clear direction, actionable priorities, and a strong connection between strategy and operational decisions.

### Portfolio Governance System

This section assesses whether the organization governs investment and prioritization decisions through explicit, repeatable, and evidence-based mechanisms.

### Product Delivery System

This section assesses whether execution is coordinated, predictable, and connected to approved portfolio priorities.

### Customer Outcomes System

This section assesses whether the organization measures meaningful value and uses outcome evidence to guide future decisions.

### Decision Intelligence System

This section assesses whether signals from across the operating model are integrated into useful leadership insight and refinement.

Together, these five sections provide a practical view of the operating system’s structural health.

## Operating Logic

The operating logic of the System Diagnostic Scorecard is based on evaluating coherence across the product leadership operating model.

1. Strategy defines direction and priorities.
2. Governance translates direction into portfolio decisions.
3. Delivery executes governed work.
4. Outcomes reveal whether value was created.
5. Intelligence integrates evidence and supports refinement.
6. The scorecard evaluates the health of each part of that system and the quality of their connections.

This logic matters because organizations often try to improve performance by focusing on one area in isolation. The scorecard is designed to show whether the overall operating model is working as a coherent system.

The scorecard therefore supports both diagnosis and prioritization by helping leaders identify where operating weaknesses are most likely to affect system performance.

## Why This Artifact Matters

Many product organizations lack a practical way to assess whether their operating model is functioning effectively.

Without a structured diagnostic tool:

- leadership discussions remain impressionistic
- operating weaknesses are identified too late
- recurring execution problems are misdiagnosed
- governance issues are confused with delivery issues
- outcome failures are treated as team problems instead of system problems

The System Diagnostic Scorecard provides a disciplined alternative. It helps leaders assess the operating model in a structured way and identify where targeted intervention is needed.

It is especially useful for:

- product operations leaders
- heads of product and strategy
- portfolio governance leaders
- transformation leaders
- executive teams evaluating organizational effectiveness and maturity

## How To Use This

Use this scorecard as a recurring assessment tool for the Product Leadership Systems Architecture.

Recommended uses include:

- baseline assessment of the current operating model
- maturity evaluation during transformation efforts
- leadership review before redesigning governance or delivery mechanisms
- structured diagnosis of strategy-to-execution breakdowns
- periodic reassessment to measure improvement over time

Recommended sequence:

1. Review the `architecture/` documents to understand the operating model.
2. Use this scorecard to assess the current state across all five systems.
3. Compare results against the maturity model in `frameworks/operating-system-maturity-model.md`.
4. Identify the lowest-scoring or most structurally important gaps.
5. Use the playbooks and diagrams to define improvement actions.
6. Reassess periodically to track operating model development.

This artifact is most valuable when used in leadership discussions that are willing to evaluate the system honestly and act on the findings.
