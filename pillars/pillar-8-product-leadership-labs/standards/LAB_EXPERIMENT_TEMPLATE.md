# Lab Experiment Template

This template defines the canonical structure for all experiment artifacts within **Product Leadership Labs**.

All experiments must follow this structure to ensure that experimentation remains comparable, reviewable, and promotable.

---

# Core Principle

> **Experiments must be structured enough to be reviewed, evaluated, and either closed or promoted**

---

# Experiment Template Structure

## 1. Experiment Name

Clear, scenario-based title.

**Format:**
```text
[SCENARIO]_EXPERIMENT
```

## 2. Problem or Opportunity

Describe the problem, constraint, opportunity, or uncertainty being explored.

Include:

- why the experiment exists  
- what gap it is addressing  
- what operating area is affected  

---

## 3. Hypothesis

Define the proposed belief being tested.

The hypothesis should explain:

- what change is being explored  
- what improvement or difference is expected  
- why the idea is worth testing  

---

## 4. Scope

Define the scope of the experiment.

Include:

- boundaries  
- affected pillar(s)  
- excluded areas  
- time horizon or experimental duration  

The scope must make clear that the experiment does not create production authority.

---

## 5. Inputs

List required inputs from existing systems.

Examples:

- strategic direction  
- governance constraints  
- delivery context  
- outcome insights  
- signals from the **Decision Intelligence System**  

Inputs must respect canonical system ownership.

---

## 6. Experiment Design

Describe how the experiment will be run.

Include:

- method  
- participants  
- cadence  
- observable signals  
- execution steps  
- exit criteria  

Design must preserve the canonical mediation path.

---

## 7. Observations

Capture what was observed during execution.

Observations should remain descriptive.

They must not:

- interpret  
- evaluate  
- assign value  
- declare success or failure prematurely  

---

## 8. Boundary Assessment

Document how the experiment preserves PLOS boundaries.

Include explicit confirmation that the experiment does not:

- bypass the **Customer Outcomes System**  
- create direct signal-to-decision paths  
- assume production authority  
- collapse system ownership  

---

## 9. Promotion Potential

State whether the artifact may be a candidate for promotion.

Include:

- target pillar, if any  
- required additional validation  
- known gaps or cleanup needed  

This section does not approve promotion. It only identifies potential.

---

## 10. Review Outcome

Document the formal review result:

- **Approved**  
- **Approved with Cleanup**  
- **Blocked**  
- **Closed Without Promotion**  
- **Candidate for Promotion Review**  

---

# Summary

This template ensures that all experiments are:

- clearly framed  
- bounded  
- reviewable  
- comparable  
- promotable only through formal process
