# Lab Promotion Model

The **Lab Promotion Model** defines how experimental artifacts within **Product Leadership Labs** move from controlled exploration into the canonical architecture of the **Product Leadership Operating System (PLOS)**.

Where Labs provides the environment for experimentation, this model defines the rules for formal adoption.

---

# Purpose

The purpose of the **Lab Promotion Model** is to ensure that no experimental artifact becomes canonical without structured validation, boundary review, and governance approval.

It exists to protect the integrity of the operating system while still enabling controlled evolution.

---

# Core Principle

> **Experimentation may generate candidates for the architecture, but only promotion creates canon**

Labs may produce promising artifacts, but those artifacts remain experimental until they pass the formal promotion path.

---

# Promotion Preconditions

An experimental artifact may be considered for promotion only if it has:

- a clearly documented hypothesis  
- defined scope and boundaries  
- completed controlled execution  
- reviewable observations  
- outcome evaluation through the **Customer Outcomes System**  
- a clear target production pillar for adoption  

If any of these are missing, the artifact is not eligible for promotion review.

---

# Promotion Path

Promotion follows a disciplined sequence.

## 1. Experiment Completion

The experiment must be complete enough to support review.

This includes:

- finalized experimental artifact  
- documented observations  
- clearly stated intended use  
- known affected systems  

## 2. Outcome Evaluation

Results must be evaluated through the **Customer Outcomes System**.

This ensures:

- evidence is interpreted in the correct layer  
- value is qualified outside the experimental layer  
- learning is generated through the canonical outcomes process  

## 3. Boundary Review

Before promotion, the artifact must be checked for:

- role leakage  
- ownership conflicts  
- alternative pathways  
- boundary collapse  
- conflict with canonical terminology and structures  

An artifact that breaks architectural boundaries must not be promoted.

## 4. Governance Decision

Governance decides whether the artifact will be:

- rejected  
- returned for further iteration  
- conditionally approved with cleanup  
- formally promoted  

Governance owns the promotion decision.

Labs may prepare and present promotion candidates, but it must not influence or predetermine governance decisions.

All promotion decisions are made independently within the Portfolio Governance System.

## 5. Pillar Integration

If promoted, the artifact is integrated into its target production pillar.

Possible targets include:

- Pillar 2 — Operating Model  
- Pillar 3 — Portfolio Governance System  
- Pillar 4 — Product Delivery System  
- Pillar 5 — Customer Outcomes System  
- Pillar 6 — Decision Intelligence System  
- Pillar 7 — Product Leadership Playbooks  

Promotion into Pillar 8 does not count as promotion, because Pillar 8 is the experimental environment itself.

---

# Promotion Outcomes

Every promotion review ends in one of four states:

## Rejected
The artifact is not suitable for adoption.

## Iterate
The artifact shows potential but requires additional experimental refinement.

## Approved with Cleanup
The artifact is valid for adoption but requires precise fixes before integration.

## Promoted
The artifact is approved and integrated into a canonical pillar.

---

# Non-Negotiable Promotion Rules

Promotion must never occur if the artifact:

- collapses evidence, meaning, and decision-making into one layer  
- bypasses the **Customer Outcomes System**  
- creates direct **Decision Intelligence System → Strategy / Governance** paths  
- assumes production authority inside Labs  
- duplicates or conflicts with existing canonical ownership  

---

# Promotion Evidence

A promotion review should include:

- experiment artifact  
- experiment summary  
- documented observations  
- outcome evaluation inputs  
- boundary assessment  
- target pillar rationale  
- required cleanup, if any  

Promotion decisions must be based on structured evidence, not informal enthusiasm.

---

# Relationship to Governance

Governance owns the decision to promote, but promotion does not redefine architectural truth.

Promotion only succeeds when governance approval and architectural integrity both hold.

A governance decision cannot legitimize boundary collapse.

---

# Relationship to Customer Outcomes System

The **Customer Outcomes System** is mandatory in promotion because it owns interpretation, value qualification, and learning generation.

No artifact should be promoted solely because signals looked promising.

Meaning must be established before promotion decisions are made.

---

# Summary

The **Lab Promotion Model** defines how experimental work becomes canonical within PLOS.

It ensures that:

- experimentation remains exploratory until validated  
- outcome meaning is established through the correct system  
- governance formally decides promotion  
- boundary integrity is preserved during adoption  

This model allows controlled evolution without architectural drift.
