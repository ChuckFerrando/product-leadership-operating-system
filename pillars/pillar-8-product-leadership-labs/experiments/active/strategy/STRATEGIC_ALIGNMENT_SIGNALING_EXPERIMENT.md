# STRATEGIC_ALIGNMENT_SIGNALING_EXPERIMENT

## 2. Problem or Opportunity

Strategic direction is often defined clearly, but alignment across teams is inconsistent and slow to propagate.

This experiment explores whether introducing structured **alignment signaling patterns** improves clarity and coordination.

Affected area:
- Strategy → Governance → Delivery alignment

---

## 3. Hypothesis

If structured alignment signals are introduced and consistently propagated, then teams will align more quickly and consistently to strategic direction.

---

## 4. Scope

- Affects: Strategy, Governance, Delivery  
- Does NOT change strategy definition  
- Duration: 1 planning cycle  

---

## 5. Inputs

- Strategic direction (Strategy)  
- Portfolio commitments (Governance)  
- Delivery alignment signals (Delivery)  
- Metrics (Decision Intelligence — signals only)  

---

## 6. Experiment Design

- Introduce structured alignment signals at planning boundaries  
- Track propagation across governance and delivery  
- Observe alignment consistency
