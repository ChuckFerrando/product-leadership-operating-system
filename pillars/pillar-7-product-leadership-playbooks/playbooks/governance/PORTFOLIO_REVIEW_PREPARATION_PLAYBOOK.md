# PORTFOLIO_REVIEW_PREPARATION_PLAYBOOK

---

## 1. Context

Triggered prior to a formal portfolio review within the **Portfolio Governance System**.

Used when:

- portfolio decisions are scheduled for review  
- prioritization or rebalancing may occur  
- leadership requires structured preparation inputs  

Involves:

- Governance (primary)  
- Delivery (execution state inputs)  
- Customer Outcomes (outcome insights)  
- Decision Intelligence (signals and visibility)

---

## 2. Objective

Ensure portfolio review sessions are prepared with complete, aligned, and system-consistent inputs to support effective governance decision-making.

---

## 3. Inputs

- Strategic direction and priorities (Strategy)  
- Current portfolio commitments (Governance)  
- Delivery status, risks, and dependencies (Delivery)  
- Outcome insights and validated learning (Customer Outcomes)  
- Metrics, dashboards, and portfolio visibility (Decision Intelligence — signals only)

---

## 4. Execution Steps

1. Gather current portfolio commitments and scope  
2. Consolidate delivery status across initiatives  
3. Surface known risks, dependencies, and constraints  
4. Compile outcome insights from Customer Outcomes  
5. Assemble supporting metrics and dashboards (uninterpreted)  
6. Structure inputs into a consistent portfolio review format  
7. Align stakeholders on review inputs and readiness  

---

## 5. Outputs

- Portfolio review input package  
- Consolidated delivery and risk visibility  
- Structured outcome insight inputs  
- Aligned stakeholder briefing materials  

---

## 6. System Interactions

### Strategy
- Consumes strategic direction  
- Does not redefine or adjust strategy  

### Governance
- Prepares inputs for governance forums  
- Does not make or influence decisions  

### Delivery
- Consumes execution status and risk information  

### Customer Outcomes
- Consumes outcome insights and validated learning  
- Does not evaluate or interpret outcomes  

### Decision Intelligence
- Uses signals and dashboards as inputs  
- Does not interpret or assign meaning  

---

## 7. Guardrails (Non-Negotiable)

This playbook must NOT:

- make prioritization decisions  
- interpret metrics or signals  
- evaluate outcome success  
- redefine portfolio scope  
- bypass Customer Outcomes  

---

## 8. Failure Modes

- Incomplete or inconsistent portfolio inputs  
- Implicit prioritization embedded in preparation  
- Misinterpretation of metrics during preparation  
- Lack of alignment across stakeholders  
- Bypassing outcome insights  

---

## 9. Signals of Execution Health

- Clear alignment on release readiness across teams  
- Reduced last-minute coordination issues  
- Consistent readiness validation across releases  
- Visibility into risks and dependencies  

(These signals may be observed via Decision Intelligence but must not be interpreted within the playbook.) 

---

## 10. Signals of Execution Health

Define observable indicators that execution is working as intended.

Examples:

- alignment clarity across stakeholders  
- reduced preparation variability  
- consistent portfolio input quality  

**Critical Rule:**
- may reference signals from Decision Intelligence  
- must NOT interpret, evaluate, or assign meaning to those signals
