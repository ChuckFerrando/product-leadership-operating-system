# RELEASE_READINESS_PLAYBOOK

---

## 1. Context

Triggered when a delivery increment is approaching release and requires coordinated readiness validation.

Involves:

- Delivery (primary)  
- Governance (commitment alignment)  
- Customer Outcomes (expected outcome alignment)  
- Decision Intelligence (signals and visibility)

---

## 2. Objective

Ensure execution readiness is coordinated, aligned, and validated prior to release.

---

## 3. Inputs

- Approved commitments (Governance)  
- Delivery execution status (Delivery)  
- Outcome expectations (Customer Outcomes)  
- Metrics and telemetry (Decision Intelligence — signals only)  

---

## 4. Execution Steps

1. Confirm alignment to approved commitments  
2. Review delivery readiness conditions  
3. Surface risks and dependencies  
4. Align stakeholders on readiness status  
5. Prepare execution and communication readiness  

---

## 5. Outputs

- Release readiness status  
- Risk and dependency visibility  
- Stakeholder alignment artifacts  
- Execution readiness confirmation  

---

## 6. System Interactions

### Strategy
- No direct interaction  

### Governance
- Consumes approved commitments  
- Does not alter decisions  

### Delivery
- Coordinates execution readiness  

### Customer Outcomes
- References expected outcomes  
- Does not evaluate outcomes  

### Decision Intelligence
- Uses signals as inputs  
- Does not interpret  

---

## 7. Guardrails (Non-Negotiable)

This playbook must NOT:

- redefine release scope  
- make governance decisions  
- interpret metrics  
- evaluate outcomes  
- bypass Customer Outcomes  

---

## 8. Failure Modes

- Misaligned readiness across teams  
- Implicit decision-making during execution  
- Interpretation of signals within playbook  
- Uncoordinated risk handling  

---

## 9. Signals of Execution Health

- Alignment across teams on readiness  
- Reduced coordination friction  
- Consistent readiness validation  

**Critical Rule:**
- signals may be referenced  
- must NOT be interpreted
